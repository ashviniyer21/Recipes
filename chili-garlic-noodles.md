# "Sweet Water" Noodles (Tian Shui Mian)
![Have not taken a picture for this recipe yet](chili-garlic-noodles.png)

## Information
[Original recipe](https://redhousespice.com/tian-shui-mian/), [Original Chili Oil Recipe](https://www.joshuaweissman.com/recipes/best-easy-chili-garlic-noodles-recipes)
<br>
**Time:** 30-40 minutes
<br>
**Servings:** 4

## Ingredients
* 16 OZ Udon / Shanghai Noodles
* 1 Block Tofu
* 1 Bell Pepper
* 2 Shallots
* 1 Cup Spinach
* 5 Cloves Garlic
* 1/4 Cup Roasted Peanuts
* 1 TSP Ground Sichuan Peppercorn
* 6 TBSP Spiced Sweet Soy Sauce
* 1 TBSP Chinese Black Vinegar
* 1 TSP Sesame Oil
* 4 TBSP Chili Oil
* 2 TBSP Sesame Paste

### Chili Oil (Makes Excess, use for other things)
* 8 Cloves Garlic
* 4 Green Onion
* 1 TBSP Sesame Seeds
* 6 TBSP Sichuan Chili Flakes
* 2 TBSP Soy Sauce
* 2 TBSP Dark Soy Sauce
* 2 TSP Seasoning Sauce (or 1 TSP MSG)
* 1 TBSP Sugar
* 2 TBSP Chinese Black Vinegar
* 1/2 Cup Neutral Oil
* 1/4 Cup Water

### Spiced Sweet Soy Sauce (Makes Excess, use for other things)
* 1 Cup Soy Sauce
* 1/2 Cup Brown Sugar
* 1/4 Cup Water
* 2 Green Onion
* 3" Ginger
* 1 TSP Sichuan Peppercorn
* 1/2 TSP Fennel Seeds
* 1 Star Anise
* 1" Cinnamon Stick
* 2 Bay Leaves
* 1 Black Cardamom

#### Spiced Sweet Soy Sauce (Lazy Version)
* 1 Cup Soy Sauce
* 1/2 Cup Brown Sugar
* 1/4 Cup Water
* 2 Green Onion
* 1 TSP Sichuan Peppercorn
* 1 TSP Ginger Powder
* 1 TSP Chinese 5 Spice

## Instructions
1. Press the tofu to be extra firm. The tofu should have as little water as possible. Preferably, start early and press the tofu for ~1/2 hr, or do this while prepping other stuff.
2. Grate the tofu into strips. It should be fairly thin, as we want to create a "crumbled tofu" effect.
3. Cut the spinach into strips. Finely mince the garlic, Dice the bell pepper and shallots. Roughly chop the peanuts.
4. Get a wok / pan on medium-high heat. Add some neutral oil. Once the oil is hot, add the tofu to fry. Stir every couple of minutes, until the tofu get crispy.
5. Once the tofu is crispy, add 2 TBSP Spiced Sweet Soy Sauce, Chinese Black Vinegar, and Sesame oil. This helps give the tofu an enhanced flavor.
6. Add the shallot & bell pepper. Cook for a couple of minutes to soften.
7. Add the garlic and sichuan peppercorn. Mix for 30-45 seconds.
8. Cook your noodles to package instructions (or slightly under, as mixing it in the dish will finish the cooking). Add the noodles to the main dish.
9. Add the remaining spiced sweet soy sauce, chili oil, sesame paste, and chopped peanuts. Mix until everything is incorporated.
10. Optionally, chop extra green onion for garnish (after the heat is turned off).

### Chili Oil
1. Finely mince the garlic and green onion. Keep the white and green of the green onion separate.
2. Add the garlic, white part of the green onion, sesame seeds, and sichuan chili flakes to a heatproof bowl.
3. Heat up the oil up to 325 F on the stove. Pour the oil into the bowl. Mix together.
4. Add all the remaining ingredients and mix together.

### Spiced Sweet Soy Sauce
1. Slice the ginger into think disks to increase surface area.
2. Combine all the ingredients, and add to a saucepan. Put the saucepan on low heat
3. Cook until the soy sauce starts to boil and thicken. The sauce should be [nappe](https://www.ciafoodies.com/kitchen-vocab-nappe/).

## Notes
* For the Chili oil, to test if the oil is hot enough, just take a tiny part of oil and see if the ingredients sizzle when poured in.
* For the lazy version of the spiced sweet soy sauce, you can also not simmer the soy sauce on the stove, and just mix everything together.
* If you're unsure if the spiced sweet soy sauce has reduced enough, it's better for the sauce to be too thin rather than too thick.
* Use 1 onion if you do not have shallots
* Spinach shrinks, so it's ok to add it whole if you don't have time. Adding it into strips helps it have a nicer texture in the final dish though.
* Toast your spices before adding it to the soy sauce (or sichuan peppercorns before adding to the final dish). This gives a nice aroma and enhances the flavor.
